# Visual Language

The visual language should balance beauty and dread.

## Direction

London Below should not be visually loud all the time. Warm apartment detail, foggy restraint, Victorian texture, shadow pressure, and carefully chosen contrast should make horror feel deliberate.

## Identity Signals

Visuals may show identity erosion through missing labels, altered objects, repeated family motifs, broken reflections, and rooms that lose personal detail.

## Accessibility

Visual horror should preserve navigation and puzzle readability unless a Director-approved moment intentionally narrows perception.

## Open Design Questions

- What color palette belongs to home before horror?
- What visual corruption represents memory loss without becoming generic glitch art?
