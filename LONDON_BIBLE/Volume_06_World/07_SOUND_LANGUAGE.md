# Sound Language

Sound should make the world feel alive, watchful, and emotionally specific.

## Core Sounds

Whispers, heartbeat, breathing, lantern pressure, rain muffling, distant knocks, fake footsteps, silence drops, family voice fragments, and room ambience may all support fear.

## Rules

- Sound must mean something.
- Familiar sounds in wrong places must be deliberate.
- Puzzle comprehension and accessibility matter.
- Final audio assets are not defined here.

## Director Connection

Audio Director should approve future sound pressure and protect safe rooms, puzzle rooms, and unknown zones.

## Open Design Questions

- Which family sound should be comforting before it becomes frightening?
- What should true silence mean?
