# London Bible

The London Bible is the creative source of truth for London Below.

London Engine documents explain how the game is built. The London Bible explains what the game means, what it must feel like, and what future chapters, monsters, puzzles, UI, audio, lighting, and narrative systems are allowed to become.

## Canon Role

London Below is an original Roblox psychological horror game about a six-year-old player with no canon name, a beautiful London apartment, Mum, Dad, Sister, Marmalade the orange cat, and a living ancient Building that consumes identity, memories, and emotional connection.

This Bible is not implementation code. It does not create Chapter 1, monsters, scares, maps, sounds, UI, or final art. It gives future work a shared canon spine so technical systems and creative content do not drift apart.

## Creative Boundary

- The game may be inspired by the polish and pacing discipline of great Roblox horror.
- It must never copy another game's maps, monsters, story, names, layouts, assets, scares, or puzzle designs.
- Nothing is random.
- Every scare must mean something.
- Every puzzle must reveal story, identity, or emotional truth.
- Jump scares are allowed only when rare, earned, and meaningful.

## Codex Usage Rules

Future Codex prompts must read the relevant London Bible files before implementing story, monsters, puzzles, chapters, UI, audio, lighting, or narrative systems.

Codex must not overwrite canon without explicit instruction from the user. Codex may add TODOs or Open Design Questions, but it must not invent final lore contradictions or silently replace established facts.

When a future task touches both engine behavior and creative intent, Codex must preserve both `ENGINE_CONSTITUTION.md` and the London Bible. Engine truth protects architecture. Bible canon protects meaning.

## How To Use This Bible

- Use `BIBLE_INDEX.md` to find the relevant volume.
- Use `CANON_STATUS.md` to distinguish locked canon from open questions.
- Use `CREATIVE_RULES.md` before writing any horror, puzzle, entity, chapter, or memory content.
- Treat unknown sections as design space, not permission to improvise contradictions.

## Current Canon Locks

- The opening is 10 to 15 minutes, not 30.
- The opening must make the player love the family before horror begins.
- The Journal is not a menu; it is the player's soul.
- Memories are fragments of identity, not coins.
- Unknown entries display as `???`.
- Identity percentage shows how much of the player remains.
- The Building existed before the main entity.
- The main entity did not create the Building; it uses it.

## Open Design Questions

- What exact year does the opening take place in?
- How much of the family history is literal, remembered, or rewritten by the Building?
- Which endings are canonically possible on first playthrough versus later playthroughs?
