# Bible Index

This index maps creative questions to the Bible files that own them.

## Volume 01: Vision

- `01_THE_VISION.md`: the overall promise of London Below.
- `02_CORE_PHILOSOPHY.md`: why the horror is emotional first.
- `03_DESIGN_PILLARS.md`: non-negotiable creative pillars.
- `04_PLAYER_EXPERIENCE.md`: what the player should feel moment to moment.
- `05_FEAR_PHILOSOPHY.md`: how fear is built and released.
- `06_EMOTIONAL_PACING.md`: the emotional arc of play.
- `07_HORROR_RULES.md`: rules for scares, tension, silence, and meaning.

## Volume 02: Story

- `01_COMPLETE_STORY.md`: full-story container with canon locks and unresolved story sections.
- `02_MASTER_TIMELINE.md`: chronology across home, family, Building, and endings.
- `03_THE_PLAYER.md`: the unnamed six-year-old protagonist.
- `04_THE_FAMILY.md`: Mum, Dad, Sister, and Marmalade.
- `05_OPENING_SEQUENCE.md`: the 10 to 15 minute home opening.
- `06_ALL_ENDINGS.md`: ending structure and replay meaning.
- `07_HIDDEN_TRUTHS.md`: truths that reframe the second playthrough.

## Volume 03: The Building

The Building is ancient, alive, and older than the main entity. These files define how it thinks, remembers, lies, and consumes identity.

## Volume 04: Entities

Entity files define the philosophy and limits of future monsters without implementing Monster AI.

## Volume 05: Gameplay

Gameplay Bible files define the emotional purpose of Journal, Memory, Identity, puzzles, objectives, replayability, and secrets.

## Volume 06: World

World files define London, districts, architecture, weather, symbolism, visual language, and sound language.

## Volume 07: Chapters

Chapter files are canon outlines, not implementation plans. They must not be treated as built content until a future chapter phase explicitly begins.

## Volume 08: Engine Integration

These files explain how creative canon should feed Observation Engine, Directors, World Intelligence, Gameplay Intelligence, Execution Bridge, future Monster AI, and future save/replay systems.
