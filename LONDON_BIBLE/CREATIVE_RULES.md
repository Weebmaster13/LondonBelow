# Creative Rules

These rules govern all future London Below creative work.

## Originality

London Below must remain original. It can admire the polish, pacing confidence, and production quality of excellent Roblox horror, but it must not copy content from any existing game.

Do not copy another game's monsters, maps, story beats, names, puzzle structure, symbols, scares, audio cues, room layouts, or visual identity.

## Meaning Before Shock

Nothing is random. Every scare, whisper, object placement, locked door, puzzle, sound, silence, and memory fragment should communicate something about identity, family, the Building, or the player's emotional state.

Jump scares may exist, but they must be rare, earned, and meaningful. A jump scare without emotional or narrative consequence is a failed scare.

## The Player

The player is six years old and has no canon name. Future writing must protect that vulnerability without becoming exploitative. The player should feel small, loved, confused, watched, and gradually erased.

## The Journal

The Journal is not a menu. It is the player's soul.

Journal entries, missing words, `???` labels, identity percentage, memory fragments, and altered text are all creative canon. UI must serve the soul metaphor instead of treating the Journal like a generic inventory screen.

## Puzzles

Every puzzle must reveal story, identity, or emotional truth. A puzzle that exists only to slow the player down should be redesigned.

Puzzles should feel like the Building is asking the player to remember, confess, choose, or lose something.

## The Building

The Building tells truth from its own perspective. It can mislead, withhold, reframe, and distort, but it should not behave like random nonsense. Players should question reality because the world has hidden rules, not because the writing is careless.

## Replay Meaning

The first playthrough tells the story. The second playthrough changes the meaning of the story.

Replay content should reveal context, not simply add collectibles. Memories should affect Journal state, Identity percentage, dialogue, endings, and future Director decisions.

## Open Design Questions

- Which symbols are reserved for family memory versus Building memory?
- How much direct dialogue should the Building use compared with environmental storytelling?
- Which kinds of scares are forbidden because they would feel cheap or cruel?
