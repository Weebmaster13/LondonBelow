# Design Pillars

## 1. Home Before Horror

The opening must earn affection. The family apartment should make players want to stay.

## 2. Identity Is Health

Identity percentage is not a score. It is the measure of how much of the player remains.

## 3. The Journal Is The Soul

The Journal must feel personal, changing, incomplete, and sometimes frightening.

## 4. The Building Has Rules

Reality bends according to hidden logic. The Building may lie by omission or perspective, but it should not behave like nonsense.

## 5. Every Puzzle Means Something

Puzzles reveal story, identity, emotional truth, or the Building's rules.

## 6. Directors Pace Horror

Major sensory, environmental, narrative, and monster moments should be coordinated by Directors, not scattered scripts.

## 7. Replay Reframes Truth

The second playthrough should change the meaning of the first.

## Open Design Questions

- Which pillar wins if replayability conflicts with emotional clarity?
- How much optional lore should be allowed before pacing suffers?
