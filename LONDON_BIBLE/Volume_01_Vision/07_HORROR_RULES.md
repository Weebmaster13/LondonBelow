# Horror Rules

## Rule 1: Nothing Is Random

Every scare must have a cause, meaning, or later explanation.

## Rule 2: Scares Must Respect Pacing

A scare that interrupts story, puzzle comprehension, or emotional payoff without purpose should be rejected by Directors.

## Rule 3: The Building Has Perspective

The Building may tell truth from its own point of view. It may not become arbitrary noise.

## Rule 4: Silence Is Active

Silence is a design tool. It can signal attention, absence, grief, or preparation.

## Rule 5: The Player Is Vulnerable

The player is six. Horror should use vulnerability carefully and meaningfully.

## Rule 6: Puzzle Horror Must Reveal

Puzzle solutions should expose memory, identity, family truth, or Building logic.

## Rule 7: The Monster Does Not Own The Game

The main entity is important, but London Below is not only about being chased. The Building, family, Journal, and identity are equally central.
