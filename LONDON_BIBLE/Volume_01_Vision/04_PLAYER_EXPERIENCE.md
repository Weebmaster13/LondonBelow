# Player Experience

The player should feel like a child moving through a world that understands them too well.

## First Ten Minutes

The opening should be safe, warm, interactive, and emotionally specific. The apartment should communicate family life through details: routines, small objects, voices, Marmalade, and quiet affection.

## Core Feelings

- Curiosity during exploration.
- Comfort before rupture.
- Unease before danger.
- Sadness after discovery.
- Fear when being watched.
- Confusion when memory changes.
- Relief that never lasts too long.

## Multiplayer Experience

Cooperation should feel like children trying not to be alone. Players may split, regroup, whisper, solve, carry memories, and protect one another, but the server owns all truth.

## Accessibility

Fear should not depend on nausea, unreadable UI, or unavoidable sensory overload. Motion reduction, clear prompts, and fair puzzle readability matter.

## Open Design Questions

- How should the game represent the child's height and vulnerability without hurting usability?
- How much voice acting should the family have in the opening?
