# Fear Philosophy

Fear in London Below comes from meaning, anticipation, and loss.

## Fear Sources

- Being watched but not chased.
- Hearing something familiar in the wrong place.
- Seeing family warmth become unreliable.
- Losing words in the Journal.
- Realizing a puzzle answer is emotionally painful.
- Discovering that the Building remembers the player differently.

## Jump Scares

Jump scares must be rare, earned, and meaningful. They should confirm or reveal a truth, not merely punish looking in the wrong direction.

## The Main Entity

The main entity should toy with players. It may watch, smile, fake leave, return, stalk, and sometimes choose not to chase. It is frightening because it appears to understand timing.

## The Building

The Building is older and deeper than the entity. It is not just a setting. It is an intelligence that consumes identity.

## Open Design Questions

- Which fear beats belong to the Building versus the main entity?
- What should the player never fully understand until replay?
