# Core Philosophy

London Below is psychological horror first.

The game should frighten players by making them care, then threatening what they care about. Scares are tools, not the point. The point is emotional erosion: memory, family, language, home, safety, and self slowly becoming unreliable.

## Core Beliefs

- Fear is strongest when it has meaning.
- Silence can be scarier than noise.
- The unknown should have rules.
- The Building should feel intelligent, not random.
- The player should feel small without feeling powerless in an unfair way.

## Player Contract

The player may be deceived, pressured, watched, or misled, but the game should remain fair. When the player looks back, they should realize the clues were there.

## Engine Relationship

Directors should protect pacing. Observation systems should help the game know when a player is alone, lost, rushing, hesitating, or emotionally exposed.

## Open Design Questions

- What is the emotional line the game should never cross?
- How direct should the game be about grief, family loss, and identity damage?
