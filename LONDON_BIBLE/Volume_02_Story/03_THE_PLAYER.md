# The Player

The player is six years old and has no canon name.

## Identity

The lack of canon name is intentional. The Building attacks identity, and the game should avoid giving the player a fixed identity that weakens that theme.

## Perspective

The player should feel small but capable. The experience should not punish players for being children; it should make the world feel large, old, and emotionally dangerous.

## Systems Connection

The player's Journal, Identity percentage, memory fragments, dialogue state, endings, and future Director decisions can all reflect how much of the player remains.

## Boundaries

Do not define a canon name without explicit user approval. Do not age up the player. Do not turn the player into a generic adult horror protagonist.

## Open Design Questions

- What personal habits does the child have before the Building begins erasing them?
- How should multiplayer represent multiple players while preserving the no-name canon?
