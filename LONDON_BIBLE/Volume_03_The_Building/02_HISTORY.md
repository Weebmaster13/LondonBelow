# Building History

The Building's history is ancient and not fully locked.

## Known Canon

- The Building existed before the main entity.
- It is alive.
- It consumes identity, memory, and emotional connection.
- It has a perspective that can differ from human truth.

## Historical Tone

The Building should feel older than any single family tragedy. Victorian London may be one visible layer, but the Building's hunger should feel deeper than one era.

## Story Function

History should explain behavior without over-explaining mystery. The player does not need a lecture; they need discoveries that make earlier rooms feel worse.

## Future Use

Historical clues may appear in architecture, documents, sound language, impossible dates, repeated names, damaged portraits, and Journal corruption.

## Open Design Questions

- Was the Building constructed, discovered, summoned, or grown?
- Which historical event first made London aware of it?
- Are there previous children, families, or investigators tied to it?
