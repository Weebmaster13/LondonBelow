# London Below AI Design

AI in London Below exists to create dread, not just damage.

## Main Monster

The main monster should feel intelligent, patient, and cruel.

It can:

- Watch from partial cover.
- Smile without chasing.
- Stalk players through fog and halls.
- Fake leave.
- Return to check hiding spots.
- Learn repeated hiding behavior during a run.
- Sometimes choose not to chase.
- Pressure isolated players differently than groups.
- React to crawlers, sound, light, objectives, and the Horror Director.

It must not:

- Be omniscient without design justification.
- Chase constantly.
- Teleport randomly for cheap scares.
- Ignore multiplayer readability.
- Copy any existing horror game's monster identity.

## Crawler AI

Crawlers are secondary pressure creatures.

They can:

- Scout rooms.
- Create noise.
- Block or pressure routes.
- Alert the main monster.
- Make players reveal themselves.

They should not replace the main monster. Their job is information flow and pressure.

## Observer System

The Observer System tracks the party.

It should watch:

- Player positions.
- Group split.
- Hiding spots.
- Repeated routes.
- Lantern use.
- Noise.
- Objective progress.
- Vulnerability.

The Observer feeds the Horror Director and AI systems.

## Building Intelligence

The building should feel aware without becoming arbitrary.

Possible reactions:

- Doors settling or locking after progress.
- Distant footsteps.
- Lights failing near vulnerable players.
- Rooms feeling different on return.
- Whispers reacting to player choices.

Building Intelligence should be coordinated through the Horror Director.

## AI Architecture

AI should be composed from modules:

- Perception.
- Memory.
- DecisionMaking.
- Behavior.
- States.
- Navigation.
- Pathfinding.
- Communication.
- Emotion.
- Learning.
- Animations.
- Utilities.

No single script should own the entire monster.
