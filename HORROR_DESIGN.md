# London Below Horror Design

London Below is psychological horror first. The goal is sustained dread, not constant noise.

## Horror Identity

- Fog, silence, wet streets, gaslight, narrow corridors, old wood, stone, and impossible building behavior.
- The main monster toys with players instead of simply sprinting at them.
- The building reacts as if it understands trespass.
- Whispers can help, mislead, repeat player actions, or imitate safety.
- Heartbeat, breathing, lantern flicker, fake sounds, silence, and tension are core systems.
- Jumpscares must be earned.

## Horror Director

The Horror Director controls pacing.

It should track:

- Party spread.
- Player isolation.
- Hiding behavior.
- Objective progress.
- Time since last pressure event.
- Deaths or near-deaths.
- Lantern use.
- Crawler alerts.
- Monster proximity.

It should output:

- Ambience changes.
- Audio pressure.
- Lighting pressure.
- Whisper timing.
- Fake sound timing.
- Crawler activity.
- Monster opportunity windows.
- Release windows.

## Fear System

Fear is not just a screen effect. It is a model of pressure.

Inputs may include isolation, darkness, monster line-of-sight, chase history, crawler alerts, whispers, hiding duration, low group confidence, and objective urgency.

Outputs may include heartbeat, breathing, subtle camera pressure, reduced certainty, whisper frequency, lantern flicker, and director escalation.

## Whisper System

Whispers should be believable and dangerous.

- Some whispers should guide.
- Some should deceive.
- Some should mimic players or safe spaces.
- Some should react to what the party has done.
- Whisper spam is forbidden.

## Jumpscare Rules

Jumpscares are allowed only when earned by pacing, player behavior, and setup. Random jump cuts without buildup are against the identity of London Below.
